About

Origin & vision

Milan Quant Capital was founded with a simple idea: bring quantitative research, automation and machine learning together in a single, cohesive trading engine.

The project started after my experience at Bocconi’s Summer School in Finance and Global Economics, where I deepened my interest in systematic trading, risk management and financial markets. Since then, I’ve been developing MQC as a live, evolving framework to test and deploy professional-style strategies on US equities.

The goal is not only to design trading rules, but to build the full infrastructure around them: data pipelines, risk controls, trade logs, monitoring and, eventually, the ability to scale to external capital.

Strategies & Stack

How MQC trades & runs

Live Strategies

The current framework runs several systematic strategies in parallel, all built around clear rules and explicit risk constraints.

  • Trend & Momentum Core
    EMA-based trend filters (20 / 50 / 200) combined with “clean breakout” momentum setups to focus on strong, directional moves only.
  • Earnings Play
    Event-driven entries around earnings dates to capture post-announcement drift and volatility, with predefined risk per trade.
  • Predictive AI Filter
    A model-based probability filter that screens entry signals and blocks low-conviction trades, adding an extra layer of validation on top of the rules.
  • ATR-based Risk Management & Sizing
    Position sizing driven by ATR, per-trade risk caps and portfolio-level limits to keep exposure aligned with volatility.
  • Rebalancing
    Automatic trimming of oversized positions back to target allocation to avoid excessive concentration.
  • Pairs Arbitrage
    Statistical arbitrage on selected equity pairs (e.g. MSFT–AAPL, XOM–CVX, META–GOOGL) using spread z-scores, beta estimation and capital allocation constraints.

Technology & Infrastructure

Milan Quant Capital is built as a fully automated Python-based stack designed to be transparent, modular and extensible.

  • Execution & Data
    Paper-trading execution via Alpaca and market data via Yahoo Finance and other APIs.
  • Strategy Engine
    Strategy logic implemented with pandas / NumPy, including filters, signals, ATR, position sizing and portfolio state.
  • Machine Learning
    Machine learning components (such as gradient-boosting-style models) used as predictive filters on top of rule-based strategies.
  • Monitoring & Logs
    Telegram integration for real-time notifications, fills and heartbeats, plus detailed decision and trade logs for post-trade analysis and research.
  • Risk & Portfolio State
    Local portfolio tracking, exposure checks and capital allocation logic to keep the engine within predefined risk boundaries.

Status & Roadmap

Where MQC stands and where it’s going

Current Status

The engine is currently running in paper trading mode, with real-time execution logic, live data feeds and full logging enabled.

  • Live multi-strategy architecture
    Momentum, earnings, AI filter and pairs arbitrage components are already integrated into a single framework.
  • Daily monitoring
    All decisions, orders and fills are tracked via Telegram and trade logs for review and refinement.

Roadmap

  • 1
    Strengthen the predictive AI engine.
    More features, deeper training and robustness checks across assets and regimes.
  • 2
    Deepen backtesting and stress tests.
    Regime-based analysis, drawdown studies and parameter robustness.
  • 3
    Expand to cross-asset and cross-market strategies.
    Beyond US equities, towards global indices, FX or other liquid instruments.
  • 4
    Prepare for external capital & live deployment.
    Hardening infrastructure, monitoring and governance for a potential transition beyond paper trading.
